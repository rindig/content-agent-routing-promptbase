# Analytics Tracker

A living document for tracking what's working, what's not, and what to do about it. Review every two weeks. The goal isn't perfect data — it's pattern recognition. You're looking for signals that tell you where to put more energy and where to pull back.

---

## The Feedback Loop (Review Every Two Weeks)

Five questions to answer every review cycle. Each one reveals something different about how your content is performing:

1. **Which hook types are getting the most initial views?**
   This tells you what stops the scroll. High initial views mean the hook is doing its job — people see the first 1-2 seconds and decide to watch. Track this by hook type (Quiet Reveal, Number Anchor, Reframe, Counter-Position, Earned Authority, Question Spiral) to see which patterns consistently earn attention.

2. **Which videos have the highest watch-through rate?**
   This tells you what retains attention. A video can get a million views but if everyone drops off at second 5, the content isn't landing. High watch-through means the script structure is working — the hook promised something, and the body delivered.

3. **Which videos drive the most profile visits?**
   This tells you what converts curiosity to interest. Profile visits mean someone watched your content and thought "who is this person?" That's the bridge between content consumer and potential audience member.

4. **Which topics generate the most comments and questions?**
   This tells you what your audience wants more of. Comments and questions are the highest-signal engagement metric. Someone took time to type a response. The questions people ask in comments are next week's topic ideas — add them to topic-engine/brainstorm.md.

5. **Which LinkedIn posts drive DMs or inquiries?**
   This tells you what drives Tier 3 pipeline. LinkedIn is the platform most directly connected to consulting and professional opportunities. DMs and inquiries are the leading indicator of revenue from content.

**Action principle:** Double down on what works. Modify what doesn't. Kill what's dead.

## Weekly Numbers to Track

| Metric | What It Tells You |
|---|---|
| Views per video | Reach — how many people saw it |
| Watch-through rate | Retention — how many people stayed |
| Profile visits from content | Interest — how many people wanted to know more |
| Follower growth rate | Audience building — is the base growing |
| DMs and inquiries | Pipeline — is content generating opportunities |
| Link clicks to products | Conversion — is content driving product interest |
| Revenue from digital products | Tier 1 health — is the base revenue layer working |
| Consulting inquiries sourced from content | Tier 3 health — is content feeding the high-ticket layer |
