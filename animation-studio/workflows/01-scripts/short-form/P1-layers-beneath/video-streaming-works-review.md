## SCRIPT

**HOOK (0-5s):**
When you watch a video online, you're actually watching thousands of tiny pieces arriving in the wrong order and being reassembled in real time.

**BUILD (5-30s):**
Here's what's happening. The video file is compressed using algorithms that remove data your eyes won't miss. It's chopped into segments, usually 2 to 10 seconds each. Each segment is encoded at multiple quality levels.

Your device requests one segment at a time. If your connection is fast, it requests the high-quality version. If it slows down, mid-stream, it switches to a lower quality. All of this happens while you're watching, invisible.

The segments arrive through different routes. Your player buffers a few seconds ahead, assembles them in order, decompresses them, and displays 30 frames per second.

**LAND (30-35s):**
You see a smooth video. Underneath, it's organized chaos. Thousands of decisions per second so you never have to wait.
