## SCRIPT

**HOOK (0-5s):**
Strip away the crypto hype and blockchain is just a very specific data structure. Here's what it actually does.

**BUILD (5-30s):**
Imagine a spreadsheet that everyone can see but no one can change past entries. Each new row gets hashed, and that hash becomes part of the next row. Change anything in an old row, and every hash after it breaks.

That's the "chain" part. Everyone has a copy. To add a new row, most copies have to agree it's valid. That's the distributed part.

It's good for exactly one thing: creating a record everyone can verify without trusting any single authority.

But it's slow. Expensive. And uses enormous amounts of energy. So for most problems where you have a trusted authority, a regular database works better, faster, and cheaper.

**LAND (30-35s):**
Blockchain isn't magic. It's a very clever solution to a very specific problem. And for every other problem, it's the wrong tool.
