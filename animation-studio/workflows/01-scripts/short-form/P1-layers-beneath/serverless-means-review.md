## SCRIPT

**HOOK (0-5s):**
When someone says "serverless," they don't mean there are no servers. They mean you don't have to manage the servers.

**BUILD (5-30s):**
Here's how it works. You write code. You upload it to a cloud provider. When someone makes a request, the provider spins up a container, runs your code, returns the result, and shuts down the container.

You pay only for the milliseconds your code actually runs. You don't manage operating systems, scaling, load balancers, or infrastructure.

But the servers are still there. In a data center. In Virginia or Oregon or Ireland. Running Linux. Using electricity. They're just someone else's problem.

**LAND (30-35s):**
"Serverless" is the latest abstraction layer. You don't see the servers, you don't manage the servers, but they're still doing all the work. Every layer hides the one beneath it. That's the pattern. Always has been.
