## SCRIPT

**HOOK (0-5s):**
Your phone predicts what word you'll type next by analyzing billions of word sequences from texts, emails, and searches.

**BUILD (5-30s):**
Here's how it works. As you type each letter, your keyboard looks at the previous 2 to 3 words you typed. It matches that pattern against a statistical model built from massive amounts of text data.

The model doesn't understand meaning. It just knows that after the words "I'll see you," the word "tomorrow" appears more often than "elephant." So it ranks suggestions by probability.

Some keyboards learn your personal patterns. If you frequently type "on my way," it starts suggesting "way" after "on my."

All of this happens in milliseconds. The model runs locally on your device, analyzing probabilities, updating rankings, every single keystroke.

**LAND (30-35s):**
It feels like your phone knows what you're thinking. It's just very good at predicting patterns. Which, honestly, is exactly what AI does too.
