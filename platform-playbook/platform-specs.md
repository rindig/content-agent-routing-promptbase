# Platform Specs

Per-platform rules for content format, posting, and optimization. Shared specs first, then platform-specific sections.

---

## Shared: Short-Form Video (TikTok, Reels, YouTube Shorts)

**Format:** Vertical 9:16, 1080x1920, 30-60 seconds animated explainers with voiceover.

**The first 1-2 seconds decide everything.** No logos, intros, or greetings. Open with the hook. First visual frame should be compelling even when paused.

**On-screen hook text:** First line of hook appears as large, readable text (upper third or center). Catches silent scrollers. Styled consistently across videos.

**Audio:** Voiceover is primary. Trending sounds only when they enhance without competing with voiceover. Never lip-sync or trend-chase.

**Hashtags:** 5-8 total. Mix broad (#coding, #ai, #tech, #programming) with niche (#softwarearchitecture, #aitools, #promptengineering, #systemdesign).

**Reply videos:** When a comment asks an interesting question, create a follow-up Short that answers it. Feeds the algorithm and generates validated content ideas.

## TikTok & Instagram Reels

**Role:** Discovery engines. Primary top-of-funnel growth channel. Algorithm pushes content to non-followers.

**Posting frequency:** 5-7 per week. Consistency > perfection. Five is the floor; seven is the target.

**TikTok specifics:**
- Bio link: Use link-in-bio tool to route to relevant offers
- Profile: Clean, professional, immediately clear what the account is about

**Instagram Reels specifics:**
- Can run up to 90 seconds (30-60 still the sweet spot)
- Algorithm also rewards saves and shares (technical explainers naturally get these)
- Re-export original file for Instagram — never cross-post with TikTok watermark
- Use Instagram Stories to tease upcoming Reels

## YouTube Shorts

**Role:** Search + discovery + long-tail library. Content compounds over months/years. Most important platform for long-term audience building.

**Posting frequency:** 4-5 per week. Slightly less than TikTok — YouTube rewards quality signals (watch-through, rewatches) over volume. Post as second-wave after TikTok/Reels performance data.

**Key algorithm difference:** YouTube Shorts heavily rewards **watch-through rate** and **rewatches**. A viewer who watches twice counts significantly more than one who watches once. Optimize for "I need to watch that again to fully get it."

**Titles — searchable first, intriguing second:**
- Lead with the searchable concept, add intrigue element second
- Good: "What Actually Happens When You Write print('Hello World') | 7 Layers Deep"
- Bad: "You Won't Believe This About Hello World"
- Include specific technical terms viewers would search for
- Include numbers or specifics when possible

**Descriptions:** 2-3 sentence summary + key terms (natural language, not keyword stuffing) + links to related videos.

**Thumbnails:** Simple text-over-animation frame. Large readable text with core concept. Consistent style across channel. High contrast for mobile readability.

**Playlists — organize by pillar** (viewer-friendly names):
- "How Things Actually Work" (P1)
- "Choosing the Right AI Tool" (P2)
- "Methods That Transfer" (P3)
- "Building Real Systems" (P4)
- "Honest Takes on AI" (P5)

**Format notes:**
- Max 60 seconds
- Include #Shorts in title or description
- Upload as standard video with vertical aspect ratio (auto-classified as Short)

## YouTube Long-Form (When Ready)

**Not the starting point.** Shorts-first strategy: build subscribers, prove topics, establish identity before long-form production begins.

**Shorts-to-Long-Form pipeline:**
1. Post Shorts across topics, track performance over weeks
2. When 3+ Shorts on a related topic all perform well, that cluster is a proven long-form candidate
3. Expand into 5-15 minute deep dive

**When long-form activates:** 4-5 Shorts/week + 1-2 long-form/month.

**Format:**
- Horizontal 16:9, 1920x1080 minimum (4K preferred)
- 5-15 minutes initially
- Animation-driven (not talking head with slides)
- Chapters via timestamps in description
- End screen: suggest related content (keep viewers on YouTube)
- No filler — 7 dense minutes > 15 padded minutes

## LinkedIn

**Role:** Professional credibility engine and decision-maker pipeline. Where Tier 3 (CTOs, VPs of Engineering, technical leadership) encounters Jake's thinking. **Highest per-follower value for consulting conversions.**

**Posting frequency:** 3-4 per week.
- 2-3 written insight posts
- 1 video clip with written context
- Optional: 1 longer-form piece (industry analysis, framework explanation) when warranted

**Content types:**
- Concise insight posts (150-300 words) — single clear idea with business/professional angle
- Framework explanations (60/30/10, abstraction layers, build-vs-buy)
- Industry analysis — more opinionated and business-focused than video platforms
- Case studies (anonymized) — naturally demonstrate consulting value
- Video clips — uploaded natively, never linked from YouTube/TikTok (5-10x reach difference)
- Longer-form pieces (500-800 words, 1-2/month) — credibility anchors

**Tone shift from video:** Same core insights, business-impact framing.
- Video: "Here's what actually happens when AI processes your prompt"
- LinkedIn: "Most organizations are misunderstanding how LLMs process context, and it's costing them in production reliability"

**Hook = first 2-3 lines** (before "see more" fold). Same intensity as video hooks.
- Bad: "I've been thinking a lot about AI adoption lately..."
- Good: "Most companies are spending 6 figures on AI tools they'll abandon within 18 months. The problem isn't the tools."

**Formatting:**
- Single-line paragraphs with breaks between them (dense paragraphs get skipped)
- Emojis: sparingly or not at all — bullet-point emojis acceptable if they aid readability
- 3-5 relevant hashtags at end: #AI #ArtificialIntelligence #SoftwareArchitecture #TechStrategy #AIAdoption

**Video on LinkedIn:** Always include written context above the video. Video posts with substantive text outperform video-only posts.

**Tagging:** Only when genuinely relevant (discussing their announcement, responding to their published work). Spam-tagging damages credibility faster on LinkedIn than anywhere else.

**Comment strategy:** Substantive 3-4 sentence comments on posts from AI/tech leaders, industry discussions, major announcements. Every comment is a micro-piece of content reaching a new audience.

**Profile:** Treat as a landing page. Headline = what Jake does and for whom. Featured section = strongest content pieces.

**LinkedIn funnel behavior:**
- TOF: Sharp insight posts that get shared widely
- MOF: Framework posts and industry analysis — builds "this person thinks clearly about my problems"
- BOF: Case studies mirroring reader's challenges — most direct path to high-value consulting
