# Tier 3 Consulting & Enterprise ($5,000+)

**Audience:** The Decision-Maker — CTOs, VPs of Engineering, IT Directors, government program managers, consultants evaluating AI strategy for their organizations.

**Principle:** These aren't products in the traditional sense. They're relationships. The content system serves as the world's longest business card — by the time a Decision-Maker reaches out, they've already consumed enough content to trust the expertise. The sales conversation is a formality. The hard work of establishing credibility happened over months of content consumption (often by someone on their team who then made the referral).

---

## 1. Enterprise AI Training ($5,000+)

Existing model from Correlation One/KPMG-style engagements. Train employees at scale with proven 95% adoption methods. This isn't theoretical — it's a format that already works and has results to show.

**What's included:**
- Customized training programs for organizations
- Curriculum adapted to the organization's industry and technical maturity
- Hands-on workshops (not slide decks)
- Proven 95% adoption methodology
- Progress tracking and reporting for leadership
- Scalable from small teams to hundreds of employees

**Pricing:** $5,000+ (scales with organization size and scope)

**Content Connection:** The content pipeline feeds this indirectly but powerfully. A CTO who watches 20 shorts already trusts you before the sales call. More commonly, a developer on their team watches the content, mentions it in a meeting, and the CTO investigates. The content doesn't sell consulting directly — it creates the conditions where consulting sells itself.

## 2. Strategic AI Audits

Help organizations evaluate where AI actually fits vs. where traditional solutions are better. This is the 60/30/10 framework applied at the organizational level. Many companies are throwing AI at problems that don't need it while ignoring problems where AI would be transformative. This service fixes that.

**What's included:**
- Assessment of current AI initiatives and their effectiveness
- Identification of high-value AI opportunities being missed
- Honest evaluation of where AI doesn't fit (and what does)
- Prioritized roadmap for AI adoption
- Risk assessment and mitigation strategies
- Executive summary and detailed technical report

**Pricing:** TBD (likely $10,000-25,000 depending on scope)

**Content Connection:** Pillar 2 "Right Tool for the Job" content demonstrates this exact expertise. When a Decision-Maker watches a video explaining why AI isn't always the answer, they think "this is the person I need evaluating our strategy" — precisely because the honesty signals trustworthiness.
