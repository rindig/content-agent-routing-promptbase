Don't overthink entries here. The point is to capture the spark before it disappears. Polish comes later.

## Expansions of Proven Winners

Ideas for applying proven formats to new subject matter. The goal is to take what worked and replicate the structure, not the content.

### Expand the T-01 "Hello World" Format

T-01 ("What happens when you write print('Hello, World!') — 7 layers deep") is the proven winner. The format works: take something seemingly simple, then peel back the layers to reveal the hidden complexity underneath. This format maps naturally to several other topics in the bank, especially across P1 (The Layers Beneath).

**Candidates for the "layers deep" treatment:**
- T-03: How the internet works — peel back from "you type a URL" through DNS, routing, packets, physical cables
- T-06: What a database does when you search — peel back from the query to indexing, storage, retrieval
- T-08: What happens in one CPU clock cycle — peel back from "it runs your code" to fetch-decode-execute
- T-09: How Wi-Fi transmits data — peel back from "you connect" to radio waves, modulation, protocols

When developing these, reference the T-01 script in `../script-lab/` for tone, pacing, and structural cues.
